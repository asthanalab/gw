Software

Software and code related to the group.

QCANT
  • QCANT is a research codebase for quantum-computing methods in chemistry, with a focus on subspace/excited-state workflows and related numerical tooling.
  • Papers:
  • P. F. Kwao, S. P. Sundar, B. Gupt, and A. Asthana, “On the generalized eigenvalue problem in subspace-based excited state methods for quantum computers,” arXiv:2503.09670 (2025).
  • A. Asthana, A. Kumar, V. Abraham, H. Grimsley, Y. Zhang, L. Cincio, S. Tretiak, P. A. Dub, S. E. Economou, E. Barnes, and N. J. Mayhall, “Quantum self-consistent equation-of-motion method for computing molecular excitation energies, ionization potentials, and electron affinities on a quantum computer,” Chem. Sci. 14, 2405–2418 (2023).
BenchmarkQC
  • BenchmarkQC is a benchmark suite for testing quantum-computing algorithms using chemically relevant and diverse correlation regimes of strongly correlated systems relevant for quantum utility. The Hamiltonainians are provided open source to allow critical testing and measuring progress toward chemically useful quantum utility.
  • Papers:
  • “Chemically decisive benchmarks on the path to quantum utility,” arXiv preprint (details/link to be added).
CFOUR
  • Coupled-cluster techniques for computational chemistry.
AutoGen
  • Open-source tools for quantum chemistry workflows.
